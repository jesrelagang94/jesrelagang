## 7. TONE & VOICE GUIDELINES

### Writing Principles
- **Professional but approachable:** Technical expertise without jargon overload
- **Problem-solving focus:** Emphasize solutions and business value, not just technologies
- **Confidence without arrogance:** "I deliver" not "I'm the best"
- **Results-oriented:** Highlight outcomes, efficiency, and measurable impact
- **Personal touch:** Maintain "I" voice to show individual accountability

### Terminology Standards
- Use "I build" instead of "I create" (more concrete)
- Use "architect" for system design contexts (professional authority)
- Use "transform" for business impact (stronger than "improve")
- Use "intelligent" for automation (implies sophistication)
- Use "production-ready" for deliverables (signals quality)
- Use "scalable" for architecture (growth-oriented)

### Avoid
- Marketing superlatives ("amazing," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge")
- Vague claims without context ("best," "fastest," "most efficient")
- Overused buzzwords ("rockstar," "ninja," "guru")
- Passive voice ("can be built" vs "I build")

## CONCLUSION

This content strategy transforms Jesrel Agang's portfolio from a designer-focused presentation to a comprehensive showcase of multi-discipline development expertise. The content emphasizes:

1. **Technical Versatility:** Clear demonstration of capabilities across automation, web, mobile, and software engineering
2. **Business Value:** Focus on solving problems and delivering measurable results
3. **Professional Authority:** Confident, specific language that establishes expertise
4. **Accessibility:** Technical enough to demonstrate competence, approachable enough to attract clients
5. **SEO Optimization:** Strategic keyword integration for improved discoverability

The phased implementation approach allows for gradual transformation while maintaining site functionality. Priority should be given to high-visibility sections (hero, about, services) before moving to supporting content.
